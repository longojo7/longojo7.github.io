My fourth week marked one month as a research apprentice and focused on completing the required training, learning about
thematic analysis, setting up MAXQDA, and completing the initial sampling and coding process. Here’s a summary of what I
accomplished:

- **Advisor Meeting:** Met for our weekly cadence to go over this week's TODOs, ask questions about the CITI training
requirements, and discuss the initial coding process.
- **CITI Training:** Completed the required CITI training for IRB compliance. Finished the Human Subjects Research course.
- **Thematic Analysis Research:** Read two articles to learn about thematic analysis and how it would be applied to our
research.
   1. [Article 1](https://blog.delvetool.com/blog/thematicanalysis)
   2. [Article 2](https://www.maxqda.com/research-guides/thematic-analysis)
- **MaxQDA Setup:** Installed MaxQDA using my advisor's paid license and familiarized myself with the platform in
preparation for coding.
- **Initial Sampling:** Randomly sampled 5 threads from r/Professors and 5 threads from r/Teachers from the AI keyword
filtered JSONL files, generated text files for each using the existing script, and uploaded all 10 files to the shared
Google Drive.
- **Initial Coding:** Began the first round of independent coding on the 10 sampled files in MaxQDA, marking and labeling
relevant text segments based on our research questions. The initial pass will likely yield hundreds of labels, and once
all three of us finish, we will sit together to define a more mature codebook and begin coding the next phase of files.

I'm really excited to dive deeper into the coding process and start finding themes within our data. Stay tuned for more updates!
