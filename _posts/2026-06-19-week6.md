My sixth week built on the initial coding phase, during which we met to review my code categorization and refine 
its structure before moving toward a final codebook. Here’s a summary of what I accomplished:

- **Categorizing Codes:** After our initial meeting last week, I was told to go back and create categories for my
codes so that I wouldn’t have 180-plus independent codes. I created about 11 buckets that I was able to place my
independent codes in, with the hope that those broader categories would be ready for finalizing our codebook.
- **Team Collaboration Meeting:** All three of us met over Zoom to review my categorization. We talked about how
my categories were too broad and that I needed to work on coming up with more subcategories within each category
to capture the differences in codes within each bucket. My categories also needed to be a bit more detailed before
we were ready to start working on the final codebook. I will be working on my subcategories, sending a draft early
next week, and meeting again to go over them.
- **Website Approval:** I received feedback from my advisor on my website, including suggestions for his section.
After making those adjustments, I got final approval, and the site is good to go.

I'm excited to dig deeper into the subcategories next week and move closer to finalizing our codebook. Stay tuned 
for more updates!
